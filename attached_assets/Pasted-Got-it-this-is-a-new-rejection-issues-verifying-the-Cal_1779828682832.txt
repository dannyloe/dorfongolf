Got it — this is a new rejection: "issues verifying the Call to Action (CTA) provided for the campaign."
This is one of the most common Twilio A2P rejections. It means the reviewers could not clearly see and verify the exact opt-in process (the "Call to Action") you described in the form when they visited your website/links.
Why This Is Happening

The SMS consent / opt-in flow on your actual site does not match or is not clearly visible to reviewers.
Reviewers visit the links you provided and expect to immediately see:
A clear description of what users are signing up for.
An unchecked checkbox for SMS consent.
Proper disclosures ("Message and data rates may apply").
Links to Privacy Policy & Terms.

Even small mismatches (wording, missing checkbox, broken links, or pages that feel empty) cause this.

From checking your site earlier:

The /sms-opt-in, /privacy, and /terms pages exist, but they appear very minimal or hard for reviewers to navigate/verify.
The main homepage doesn't prominently show the consent flow.
